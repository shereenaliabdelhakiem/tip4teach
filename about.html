You might talk about your origin story,your team,highlight awards or recognition,or share photos. Tap into your creativity. You’ve got this. This way you tell your story online can make all the difference.

Don’t worry about sounding professional. Sound like you. There are over 1.5 billion websites out there, but your story is what’s going to seperate this one from the rest. If you read the words back and don’t hear your own voice in your head, that’s a good sign you still have more work to do.

Teachers are a hub of community-based solutions

In addition to the countless roles that teachers perform outside of their job descriptions, they are frequently the hub of community-based solutions.

In 2021, our home burned down, and as tragic and painful as it was the most extraordinary silver lining experience came from teachers providing our children with emotional and social support during this critical period. One teacher took it upon herself to provide backpacks and school supplies for each of our kids; another, having taught our son the previous year and knowing him personally, bought an entire set of Harry Potter novels; another provided a tranquil classroom for our daughter, who struggled with the emotional toll the event had during her formative years.We never asked any of them for support, yet they gave freely.

The greatest Hallmark story came in the form of the High School PE teacher, Maddie Williams. In addition to teaching our two daughters when this event occured, Ms Williams served as the head volleyball coach. Coach Williams organized a clothing drive to help our entire family, and the players wrote letters of support to us. Their generosity blew us away. I believe that her leadership molds the lives of her players beyond the volleyball court, and they gain a greater sense of self, which comes with serving others. Coach Williams performed an utterly selfless act and, in so doing, lifted our family in our time of need and taught her players the value of community service. I don’t believe it was a coincidence that the team went on to win the Texas 6A State Championship. I am forever grateful for the support and love our family received from these teachers and students.
